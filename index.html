"Ciao!"
My name is Francesca and I am from Sardinia, Italy.

Today I'm going to talk to you about the island I am from and where my family, friends and my heart are.
Here a list of some of my favorite places to visit while there:

  1. Cagliari
  2. Barumini
  3. Oristano
  4. Alghero
  5. Bosa
  6. Baunei
  7. Cala Luna
  8. Villasimius
  9. La Maddalena

Cagliari

Cagliari is the capital of Sardinia.
It's located about 30 minutes from Furtei, the village where I was born and raised. In Cagliari there is the university where I studied (Università degli Studi di Cagliari).
It's a beautiful city, you can walk everywhere, there are so many shops and little restaurants.

My favorite things to do in Cagliari are:

  • eating an ice cream (pistachio is my favorite flavour) while sitting in front of the port.
  • drinking a cocktail at a place called Biffi.
  • swim in the clear waters of the Poetto beach

Barumini

Barumini is very close to my village. It's known for Su Nuraxi, which was declared UNESCO World Heritage Site.
It's the most impressive and the most well-preserved inheritance left to us by the Nuragic civilization.
I visited it so many times, the history of this place is full of mistery and that's what fascinates me the most.
If I could travel back in time I would go back to the Nuragic era.

Golfo di Oristano

Ahh the Golfo di Oristano, full of history and beautiful landscapes.
Oristano is famous for the Mont'e Prama giants, incredible sculptures created by the Nuragic civilization.

Things I love to do in the Golfo di Oristano:

  • going to the is Arutas beach
  • drinking vernaccia
  • visiting Michela, my best friend

Alghero

I love walking through the streets of Alghero! This city is there since pre-historic times.
The Ozieri culture was present here in the 4th millenium BC (Necropolis Anghelu Ruju) and the Nuragic civilization settled there around 1500 BC.
This city was also colonized by Catalans and the population still talks using the Catalan dialect (it is now recognized as a minority language).
Alghero is also famous for the coral, there are multiple shops that sell beautiful (and expensive) coral jewelry.

Bosa

Bosa is so colorful! It was named best historical center of Italy a few years ago.
When there you can visit the Serravalle Castle. Bosa is also known for the Malvasia, a sweet and fruity wine.

Baunei

If you go to Sardinia you cannot not go to Baunei! If you like hiking you should go to Cala Goloritzé.
This beautiful beach was closed to traffic of motorboats to preserve it from pollution and assault of tourists,
now it's only accessible walking for one hour and a half or by the sea (you can rent a boat then swim for 200 meters (650 feet)). If you hike there make sure to bring water!!

Cala Luna

Cala Luna is beautiful, you can go there by boat or hiking.

Villasimius

I worked in Villasimius for three summers in a bar/gelateria and, beside working and going to the beach, I had the privilege to eat all the gelato that I wanted!
This is such a pretty village, it'a also called "the pearl of the south" in the summer every night there is a market in the main street called "via del mare".

La Maddalena

La Maddalena lends its name to the whole archipelago, of which it is the largest island, and the national park of which it is the administrative centre.
Things I like to do in La Maddalena:

  • rent a boat and explore the small islands of the archipelago
  • drink a cocktail at a place called The Duke
  • going to Caprera and visit Garibaldi's house
  • walk around the village
